Salvatore Nunnari

Associate Professor of Economics at Bocconi University

Research Fellow at CEPR and CESifo

Advisory Editor at Games and Economic Behavior and Editorial Board Member at Political Science Research and Methods

I earned a Ph.D. in Social Sciences from the California Institute of Technology in 2012 and have previously served as Assistant Professor at UC San Diego and Columbia University. At Bocconi, I am also the Director of the Bocconi Experimental Laboratory for the Social Sciences, and the B.Sc. in Economic and Social Sciences. My research is in political economy, behavioral economics, experimental economics, and microeconomic theory, and it has been published in leading international journals including the American Economic Review, the Journal of the European Economic Association, Management Science, the American Political Science Review, the American Journal of Political Science, and the Journal of Politics. I am the Principal Investigator of a European Research Council grant on the “Behavioral Foundations of Populism and Polarization”.
